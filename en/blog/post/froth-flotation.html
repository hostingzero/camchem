Best Chemicals for Froth Flotation

Tina Wu - January 06, 2022

What is Froth Flotation?

Froth floatation is the process that involves separating hydrophobic substances from hydrophilic ones. It is a method that was first used by the mining industry. After many years, it was adopted for mineral processing, waste-water treatment, and paper recycling. The process is considered one of the most successful technologies of the 21st century.

The process enhances the recovery of valuable minerals during the mining process. It is the most effective method for upgrading and recovering Sulfide ores. This process can also retrieve important minerals such as lead-bearing and copper-bearing minerals. Mechanized mining has also been adopted to get good-grade metals from lower-grade ore.

The froth floatation process involves the use of many chemicals. There are chemicals known as collectors whose function is essentially to trap ores. The collectors are mostly anionic sulfur ligands and Xanthate salts.

There is also another group of chemicals called Frothers. They are responsible for stabilizing the foam in the floating process. The Frothers can be pine oil, polyglycols, or alcohol.

Another set of chemicals called modifiers is used to optimize the separating process. The modifier chemical reacts with the collector to enhance the floatation response. Common modifiers are caustic soda, hydrochloric acid, phosphate, carbonate, starch, and glue.

Activated Carbon.

Activated Carbon is known as a group of porous Carbon. It mostly appears in a crystalline black color that makes it look like charcoal. Although the appearance sometimes depend on the process of the activation. It has varying mesh sizes also. The chemical is produced from sources that are rich in Carbon.

Chemical application of Activated Carbon

Activated Carbon is a particularly efficient chemical compound in the chemical industry due to its wide variety of industrial applications. Its main applications and strengths are:

  • It is an active chemical used in the cyanidation process, which involves mining gold from ore containing cyanide.
  • It is also used to remove contaminated agents from liquids and gasses. It can be used for water purification and to remove pollutants from industrial gasses.
  • It is further used in the various methods of gold extraction as it is very good for easier gold adsorption.

4. Calcium Carbide

Calcium Carbide.

Calcium Carbide is also known as Calcium Acetylide. It is an inorganic compound with the chemical formula CaC2. The chemical appears colorless in its pure form but has a brown or gray color in its technical grade form. It has an unpleasant odor, similar to garlic odor. The chemical reacts violently with water.

Chemical Applications of Calcium Carbide

Calcium Carbide is a particularly efficient chemical compound in the chemical industry due to its wide variety of industrial applications. Its main applications and strengths are:

  • In froth floatation, it is used with calcium phosphide to float and ignite naval signal flares.
  • It is efficient in desulfurizing irons. It can also act as a fuel to change the scrap ratio to liquid iron.
  • It is used as an effective deoxidizer in ladle treatment facilities.
  • It can act as a mole repellant and determine the amount of moisture in the soil.
  • Calcium Carbide reacts with Nitrogen to produce Calcium cyanamide.
