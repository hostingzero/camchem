What is Chloroprene Rubber?

Tina Wu - June 01, 2024

Chloroprene Rubber is an industrial chemical made up of the elements carbon, hydrogen, and chlorine. Chloroprene Rubber is also known by the trading name of Neoprene Rubber. It is a lumpy matter that appears as milky white to slightly yellowish in color. The compound comes in the forms:

  • Chloroprene Rubber CR-2322
  • Chloroprene Rubber CR-2442
  • Chloroprene Rubber CR-2443
  • Chloroprene Rubber CR-2444.

This chemical has many industrial uses but largely it is used as a raw material for the production of various adhesives in the adhesives and sealants industry.

Chloroprene Rubber Uses

 

 

Chloroprene Rubber is an extremely useful chemical. It is a special use chemical most popularly used in the adhesives and sealants industry. Its uses are:

  • Chloroprene Rubber is used as a good general use rubber.
  • It is used as a raw material in the production of adhesives.
  • It is used in the production of adhesive cement.
  • It is largely used in the production of adhesives used in the shoe industry.
  • It is used in wire and cable insulations.
  • It is used in the making of hoses.
  • It is used to make belts.
  • Chloroprene Rubber is also widely used for making gaskets.
  • It is also suitable for use in rubber, metal, leather, wood, and construction materials.

Is Chloroprene Rubber Safe?

 

 

Chloroprene Rubber is considered safe because the chemical is non-toxic. Exposure to this chemical does not have any acute or chronic health effects. According to the MSDS of CR-2332, MSDS of CR-2442, MSDS of CR-2443, and MSDS of CR-2444, it can cause irritation to the eyes, nose, and throat because of

fumes produced by heating at temperatures above 200Â°C. this exposure to fumes may cause redness, tearing, and itching of the eyes and soreness in nose and throat along with coughing. Proper storage and disposal of Chloroprene Rubber needs to be taken into consideration in order to avoid any unwanted effects.

How is Chloroprene Rubber Made?

Industrially, Chloroprene Rubber is made by polymerization of Chloroprene. The chlorination of butadiene or isoprene results in the formation of chloroprene. It is then emulsified in water and polymerized to process chloroprene into rubber.
