How To Successfully Import Chemicals To The Philippines

Tina Wu - June 11, 2023

Introduction to the Philippines Chemical Market

Importing chemicals into the Philippines is a business opportunity for people who may want to make a living out of the chemical industry. China is well known for its abundant resources, particularly in chemical production, which aids easy accessibility to chemicals at a low price. To successfully import chemicals into the Philippines, you have to know the basic steps involved in the importation process.

This article will provide all the necessary information on importing chemicals into the Philippines. We've discussed the step-by-step process of importation. You have everything needed to know about importing chemicals into the Philippines at your fingertips. Dig in!

 

The Philippines' chemical market is quite diverse and has the potential to grow significantly. The chemical industry of the Philippines is one of the nation's largest manufacturing sectors, with about 11% share of the total Manufacturing Value Added, and supplies other necessary sectors, including healthcare, food processing, and agriculture with products. The Philippines chemical market has chemical products ranging from fertilizers, soaps, and paints, with over 46,000 chemical substances registered in PICCS (Philippines Inventory Of Chemical and Chemical Substances).

How Can I Import Chemicals To The Philippines From China?

The Philippines is a booming chemical import market with a significant growth possibility. You can import chemicals into the Philippines in two ways; by sea or air. China and the Philippines are well-connected via sea and air routes. There is a regular flow of sea and air traffic between both countries. The shipping rates might depend on the route you decide to take and the quantity of the chemicals you're importing. However, shipping takes much longer than air freight transit time. There are basic documents that you would require once your goods have landed in the Philippines. They include an Ocean Bill of Lading or Air Waybill, Commercial Invoice, Packing list, Import Permit, Certificate of Origin, Customs Import Declaration, and a Product Registration Certificate.

To import your chemicals into the Philippines from China, you'd need the services of a forwarding and shipping agent. This area is quite competitive, making the customer king. You can easily choose the best company and select your rates.

How To Import Chemicals to The Philippines From China: Step By Step

Importing chemicals into the Philippines from China is one sure way to increase your profits and gain access to chemicals easily. Here are the basic step-by-step instructions on importing chemicals into the Philippines;

Register

The first and one of the most important steps in importing chemicals into the Philippines is to register yourself as a lawful importer with the necessary government departments and agencies. The Bureau of Internal Revenue will provide you with an Import Clearance Certificate after following due process.

You must register your business with the Bureau of Customs, where you'll open an account. The clearance certificate lasts for three years, while the registration at the Bureau of Customs requires it to be renewed yearly. The renewal takes about 15 days. 

 

Finding the Right Supplier

The importation process begins with finding the right supplier to provide you with the chemicals you need at the price and quantity you want. You can visit China and find a suitable factory, or you can easily get one online. You can also get a supplier through the trade shows China hosts occasionally; just visit any supplier representing chemicals and search for a suitable supplier there.

Negotiate the Price

Once you find a suitable supplier, you can start trading based on orders. This is when you negotiate the chemical quality, payment terms, price, delivery time, and incoterms. It would be best to have everything figured out before you move on to the next stage.

Place An Order

Once you've agreed to all the terms and conditions with your supplier, you can place an order officially. This usually happens after you've paid 30% of the order or after both the seller and buyer have agreed. You can pay the advance payment through bank transfer, L/C, T.T., or whatever means you and the seller agree upon.  

Find a Shipping Agent

As you're nearing completing your order, you must arrange a freight company that'd handle the shipment delivery to the Philippines. You get quotes from them and can decide which gives the best rates and terms.

Payment Balance And Delivery

After completing your order, you ask your designated freight company to contact your supplier and pick up your chemicals from the warehouse or factory there. After paying your balance to the supplier, your products will be picked up and shipped from China to the Philippines. The freight company would go through the formalities at the Chinese customs as both parties agreed upon, then ship the products to the Philippines.

Summary

Importing Chemicals from China into the Philippines can increase your profitability while accessing a diverse and wide range of Chemicals at an affordable price. Importing chemicals into the Philippines from China helps boost your profits greatly while giving you access to a wide and diverse range of chemicals. Due to the nature of chemicals, it requires proper handling, so several procedures must be completed before you can successfully import and collect your chemicals at any Philippines port.
